The heliocentric model triggered a revolution, the Copernican Revolution, that led to more work and many more discoveries in the field of astronomy over the next one century. Johannes Kepler (1571 - 1630), a German scientist, concluded that planets moved in elliptical orbits and not in circular or epicyclic ones. Kepler's Three Laws of Planetary Motion endorsed the physical reality of the Copernican model and corroborated it with real astronomical data.
