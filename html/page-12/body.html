Galileo Galilei (1564 - 1642), the father of modern observational astronomy and father of science, discovered the Phases of Venus through his telescope and documented Jupiter's Galilean Moons for the first time. His observations supported the Copernican cosmology over its predecessors.

The Copernican Revolution came to an end only when Sir Isaac Newton (1642 - 1726) formulated the Law of Universal Gravitation and his famous Three Laws of Motion. Gravitation removed the last doubts about the validity of the heliocentric model of Solar System.
