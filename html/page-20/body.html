Thus value of \(R_☉ \) can be assumed negligible and value of \( {R_⊕ + R_☉} \approx {R_⊕} \). In other words we can assume that the Sun is fixed at the C.O.M and Earth is orbiting around it for all its worth.

Substituting for values of mass, velocity and distance of Earth from the Sun, we get:

\(F_c\) = 3.57 x 10^19 N.

The Gravitation pull on Earth \( F_g \) = \( \frac{(G)(M_⊕)( M_☉)}{R_⊕^2} \)

Substituting for the values of the Gravitational Constant G and masses of both Earth and Sun, and we'll get:

\( {F_g} \approx 3.57 \) x 10^19 N


Thus \( {F_c} = {F_g} \)
