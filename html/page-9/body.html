It took more than a thousand years and courage to figure out what our Solar System looks like, and how it works. Up until the sixteenth century only five planets were known to man. People believed that Earth was at the center of the Universe and Moon, Mercury, Venus, Sun, Mars, Jupiter and Saturn turned around it in succeeding celestial spheres. Strictly in that order.

This was the Geocentric model, see visual overleaf, that ancient astronomer Ptolemy (c. 87-150 AD) postulated almost 1500 years before Copernicus arrived. Copernicus also put planets in their correct order i.e. Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter and Saturn.
