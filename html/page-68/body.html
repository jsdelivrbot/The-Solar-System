Polarity Reversal

Since we looked at the tennis racket theorem recently, it is a good time to learn about another kind of flip that happens on Earth: Polarity reversal. The Earth's magnetic field, powered by its rotating molten iron core known as geodynamo, has flipped polarity many times in the past. This is also called Geomagnetic Reversal which flips our North and South poles.

Studies of artifacts and rocks on Earth show that polarity reversals have happened every few hundred thousand years and the last one occurred ~786,000 years ago. Scientists believe that the next geomagnetic reversal is long due and it should happen soon. Observations suggest that Earth's magnetic field has weakened by over 5% in the last one century. It is thought that during a polarity reversal Earth's magnetic field weakens by over 90% before reappearing on the other side again.

According to NASA, during a polarity reversal there isn't a likelihood that the magnetic field would disappear completely.
