The surface of the Sun is called Photosphere, followed by Chromosphere and then Corona. Temperatures on the Sun vary greatly ranging from about 3800-5800K in the Photosphere to roughly 15.7 million K at the core.

In addition to heat and light, the Sun also emits a stream of charged particles (electrons and protons) known as the Solar Wind. These high energy particles cause spectacular effects called the Aurora on Earth, especially at the magnetic poles.
